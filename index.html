Bloom
Delilah S. Dawson (2023)
~190 pages Debut novel: No Avg rating: 3.8★
Theme fit
5 / 5
CW Heavy

Rosemary “Ro” Dutton, a newly tenured literature professor, falls into a whirlwind romance with Ash, a charismatic artisan whose perfect cottagecore life hides dangerous secrets. As their relationship deepens, Ro’s world narrows to fit Ash’s idyllic orbit and the line between love, obsession, and something inhumanly hungry blurs in a slow, creeping folk‑horror spiral.

Content warnings: Psychological horror, gaslighting, obsession, manipulation, implied supernatural violence, toxic relationship dynamics, body‑centric horror.

Things Have Gotten Worse Since We Last Spoke
Eric LaRocca (2021)
~120 pages Debut book: No Avg rating: 3.9★
Theme fit
5 / 5
CW Extreme

Told entirely through emails and instant messages, this online‑relationship horror tracks two lonely women whose kink‑heavy dynamic curdles into exploitation and cruelty. The more they feed each other’s worst impulses, the more their private fantasies leak into real‑world harm, making “obsession” feel less like a mood and more like a trap they built together.

Content warnings: Graphic sexual violence, extreme sadomasochism, self‑harm, emotional and psychological abuse, homophobia, animal death, gore, financial exploitation, disturbing kink and bodily degradation.
