Novel
Slewfoot: A Tale of Bewitchery
Brom (2021)
~330 pages Debut novel: No Avg rating: 4.2★
Theme fit
5 / 5
CW Extreme

Vibe: A lush, folk‑horror witch tale that feels like a dark fairytale about the forest fighting back.

In seventeenth‑century New England, a young woman pushed to the margins of her Puritan village begins to forge a dangerous bond with an ancient horned spirit in the woods. As suspicion of witchcraft rises and cruelty from church and husband escalates, the line between devil, god, and defender blurs, turning the story into a bloody, morally thorny battle over who truly deserves to be hunted.

Content warnings: Witchcraft, pagan ritual, animal death, gore, body horror, patriarchal abuse, attempted sexual assault, religious violence.

Novel
The Year of the Witching
Alexis Henderson (2020)
~360 pages Debut novel: Yes Avg rating: 4.0★
Theme fit
5 / 5
CW Extreme

Vibe: A dark feminist witch novel that feels like The Handmaid’s Tale dropped into a cursed, blood‑soaked forest.

In the rigidly patriarchal, theocratic community of Bethel, young outcast Immanuelle learns that her dead mother was tied to forbidden witchcraft and the blighted woods beyond the town. As she’s drawn into pacts with the witches who haunt that forest, plagues and omens begin to attack Bethel, forcing her to decide whether to protect the people who’ve oppressed her or let the old magic tear their world apart.

Content warnings: Religious trauma, sexism, racism, domestic abuse, cult‑like religion, gore, witchcraft, child death.

Novelette
The White People
Arthur Machen (1904)
~70 pages Debut book: No Avg rating: 3.8★
Theme fit
4 / 5
CW Heavy

Vibe: A dense, uncanny classic that feels like reading a pagan diary of initiation into forbidden rites.

Framed by a philosophical discussion about the nature of true evil, the story presents a young girl’s secret “Green Book” diary describing her hidden games and rituals in the countryside. As her innocent language slowly reveals deeper involvement with strange beings and occult practices, the tale becomes a slow, disorienting descent into witchcraft seen through the eyes of someone who barely realizes what she’s joined.

Content warnings: Occult ritual, child endangerment, psychological horror, religious anxiety, implied witchcraft.

~45 pages Debut book: No Avg rating: 3.8★
Theme fit
5 / 5
CW Extreme

Vibe: A cosmic‑math‑meets‑witchcraft tale that feels like a fever dream about geometry opening onto hell.

Walter Gilman, a student obsessed with advanced mathematics and folklore, rents a notorious attic room in Arkham once occupied by accused witch Keziah Mason. His increasingly vivid dreams pull him into her rituals, strange angles, and otherworldly journeys, blurring sleep and reality until he’s forced to confront whether witchcraft and weird geometry are simply legends—or tools for contacting something far worse.
